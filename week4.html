Week 4

We continued with the development of the object we are working with, starting the week with a Transition Map and some experiments we had with p5js.
The Transition Map helped us to identify how the product should work and function based on the input and output of the user.


▼

[Sketches to be included]
We started to put together the broom parts one by one, first addressing the wires of the LED and micro bits first.

▼

As we expect the user to interact with the broom effortlessly, we realised using a bread board to house the wires would be too bulky for the broom to function smoothly. Hence, we resort to soldering the LED and microbit wires to ensure that the components could be better contained.

▼

After soldering the LED strip and the microbit, we went ahead to slot the wires through for the pole assembly to be done.

▼

Very quickly, we went ahead to pull the LED strip through the white acrylic pole which houses and turns the strip into a softer lighting. The idea of a softer lighting also helps to reduce the initial glare users would experience with bare LED strips.

▼

After housing the LED strip through the acrylic pole, we went ahead to solder the speaker which would allow sound to be played when the pole is moved.

▼

▼

This followed our progress for the week and we would continue to work on improving the broom's experience.
