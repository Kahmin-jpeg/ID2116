Week 6 | Recess Week

For the current week, we focused a lot on integrating the various component our object consists.
From finalising the microbits neopixel code to identifying the rightful approach to encapsulate the electronics. Through the consultation session we had with Yuta,
we learned that our initial transition map did not specifically showcase the output we proposed, hence we made a revision to simplify
the transitions so we could minimise potential confusion during the making of our Broom.


▼

We made a couple of changes with the construction of encapsulating the components, housing a total of a microbit, a button and a battery.

▼

As for the software aspect, we made a few discoveries while translating our transition map into reality. Theoritically, we would expect to achieve the red string lights with 0 and +180 degree (to simulate free-fall), however when we experimented with the code, we uncovered that 0 and -180 degree triggers the red string of lights instead.

▼

The exploration quickly went on to looking at the assembly of the product to test how it'll turn out.

▼

▼

Revisiting our transitioning map, we realised that we are required to connect to p5.js via Bluetooth as we displaced the idea of having a physical speaker. However, when Neopixel is part of the equation, linking p5.js via bluetooth cannot be done, this is when we introduced another Microbit to the initial equation.

▼

To connect the additional microbit, it would mean that a new line would be added to our microbit code. This is when 'serial' is being introduced to us.

▼

As for p5.js interation alone, we managed to get it working. However, we are still trying to figure out how are we able to connect the microbit 1 to microbit 2 and then to p5.js.
